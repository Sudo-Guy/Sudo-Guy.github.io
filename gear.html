Gear system (not a shopping list)

The goal isn’t “buy everything.” It’s to build a layering + traction + safety system that matches conditions. This page is ready for brand/model notes, links, and your personal gear tiers.

Cost-effective rule: Spend money where failure is dangerous (warmth, traction, navigation, emergency). Save money on convenience items until you know your real needs.

Layering framework

  • Base layerMoisture management; avoid cotton. Document your “go-to” weights.
  • Active insulationWarmth while moving; breathable midlayer strategy.
  • ShellWind + snow protection; when to prioritize breathability vs weatherproofing.
  • Belay / stop layerPut on immediately when stopping; your “don’t get cold” insurance.

Traction ladder

  • SpikesLow-angle ice / packed snow. Note your “default carry” conditions.
  • CramponsSteeper / firm conditions. Add fit notes and boot compatibility.
  • Ice axeSelf-arrest and steep snow travel. Add training + when it’s mandatory.
  • SnowshoesFlotation + trail breaking. Add local rules/ethics and when to bring.

Ready-to-fill: my personal gear tiers

Fill these in with your actual items once you’re ready.

  • Tier 1 — “Starter but safe”Minimum kit that covers most beginner winter hikes.
  • Tier 2 — “More objectives”Upgrades for longer routes, higher winds, colder stops.
  • Tier 3 — “Steep snow / winter climbing”Tools + training prerequisites; stricter conditions checks.
