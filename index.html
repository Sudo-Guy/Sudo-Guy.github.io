How to start winter hiking and winter mountaineering—safely, confidently, and without overspending.

I’m building this site to document the exact approach I use to prepare for winter trips: the systems, gear decisions, and risk checks that keep things safe and repeatable. The goal is to make it easier to bring friends and climbing partners into winter objectives with a clear plan and a shared safety mindset.

Start with Planning Build a Gear System Safety Framework
Important: This site is not a substitute for professional instruction or local guidance. Winter conditions can be serious. Use conservative judgment, verify conditions, and turn around early.

Why this matters to me

Winter hiking and mountaineering can feel inaccessible—expensive gear lists, unclear safety practices, and “you just have to know” culture. This project turns my learning process into a practical guide so that partners can ramp up safely and cost-effectively.

Who it’s for

Primarily friends and climbing partners I want to bring on winter trips—plus anyone who wants a structured path into winter hiking, winter peaks, and beginner winter climbing fundamentals.

  • NewcomersBuild foundational systems (layers, traction, pacing, navigation).
  • PartnersStandardize communication, turn-around rules, and emergency habits.
  • Budget-focused hikersPrioritize purchases and avoid expensive mistakes.

How to use the site

Start with Planning, then review Gear and Skills. Before every trip, use the Safety page as a pre-departure checklist. The Resources page is where I’ll keep links, readings, and templates.
