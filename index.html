How to start winter hiking and mountaineering safely, confidently, and without overspending.

I built this site as a working guide for the way I prepare for winter trips: planning first, gear second, skills practice always, and safety checks before leaving the trailhead.

Start with Planning Compare Gear Systems Review Safety
Important: This site supports learning, but it does not replace professional instruction, local knowledge, or conservative judgment in winter conditions.

Why this matters to me

I want this website to feel like a real trail partner's notebook, not a generic checklist. A lot of winter advice online is either too vague or too expensive. My goal is to collect the systems that have helped me think more clearly about weather, layers, pacing, and partner readiness.

About the author

I am building my own winter hiking and mountaineering framework as I gain experience, refine my gear choices, and prepare to bring friends into colder, higher-commitment objectives.

  • What I focus onTrip planning, budget-conscious gear choices, and repeatable partner systems.
  • Why I made thisTo turn my notes and lessons learned into a resource that feels personal and usable.
  • What I am still buildingMore route examples, field photos, and real trip checklists as the site grows.

How to start

Begin with route planning and weather checks. Build a conservative objective, then match your clothing, traction, and emergency systems to that plan.

Why this matters

Winter mistakes compound quickly. A simple framework helps reduce rushed decisions, missed gear, and partner miscommunication before those problems become serious.

How to use the site

Move through the site in this order: Planning, Gear, Skills, Safety, then Resources. The menu follows that same sequence so the home page and navigation now match.
